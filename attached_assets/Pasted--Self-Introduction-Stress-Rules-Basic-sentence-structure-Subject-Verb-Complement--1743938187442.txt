### ▪ Self-Introduction & Stress Rules

- Basic sentence structure: Subject + Verb + Complement
- Focus on the first-person singular (*eu*)
- **Stress rules:**
  - Default stress on the second-to-last syllable (paroxytone)
  - Accent marks indicate exceptions (e.g., *você*, *café*)
  - Nasal syllables: *ão*, *em*, *im, etc.*
- Pronunciation: *r/rr*, *s/z*, *lh*, *nh*

### ▪ Prepositions and Contractions

- Core prepositions: *de, em, a, para, com*
- Common contractions with *eu* context:
  - *de + a = da*, *em + a = na*, etc.
- Location and origin using *eu*: *Eu moro em..., Eu sou de...*

### ▪ Regular Verbs – Present Indicative

- Verb classes: *-ar, -er, -ir*
- Present tense: first-person only
  - *falar → falo*
  - *comer → como*
  - *abrir → abro*
- Negative and question forms with *eu*

### ▪ Verbs *ser* / *estar*

- First-person singular forms:
  - *ser → eu sou*
  - *estar → eu estou*
- Usage distinctions in the *eu* form

### ▪ Irregular Verbs – Present Tense

- First-person forms only:
  - *ter → eu tenho*
  - *ir → eu vou*
  - *fazer → eu faço*
  - *poder → eu posso*
  - *querer → eu quero*
  - *dizer → eu digo*
  - *dar → eu dou*
  - *vir → eu venho*

### ▪ Pretérito Perfeito – Regular Verbs

- First-person singular only:
  - *falar → falei*
  - *comer → comi*
  - *abrir → abri*
- Negation and questions in the past with *eu*

### ▪ Nouns, Articles, and Agreement

- Gender (masculine/feminine) and number (singular/plural)
- Definite articles: *o, a, os, as*
- Indefinite articles: *um, uma, uns, umas*
- Adjective agreement with first-person subject (*Eu sou brasileiro/a*)

### ▪ Pretérito Perfeito – Irregular Verbs

- First-person singular forms:
  - *ser/ir → fui*
  - *ter → tive*
  - *fazer → fiz*
  - *poder → pude*

### ▪ Periphrastic Future

- Structure: *ir (present) + infinitive*
  - *Eu vou estudar, Eu vou sair*
- Usage: near-future, plans, intentions with *eu*

### ▪ Demonstratives and Possession

- Demonstratives: *este, esse, aquele* (focused on *eu* context)
- Possessive adjectives: *meu/minha*
  - *Meu nome é Gil. Minha casa é aqui.*

### ▪ Plurals and Agreement Review

- Plural noun/adjective forms (for reading and comprehension)
- Reinforcement of agreement when *eu* is the subject

### ▪ Interrogatives and Questions

- Question words: *o que, onde, quando, como, por que*
- Yes/no and WH- questions in the *eu* form:
  - *O que eu faço? Onde eu moro?*

### ▪ Imperative Mood (Commands)

- Affirmative: *fala, come, abre* (2nd person)
- Negative: *não fale, não coma, não vá*

### ▪ Reflexive Verbs and Pronouns

- Reflexive pronouns: *me, se, nos*
- Conjugation in other persons: *você se chama...*

### ▪ Introduction to the Imperfect Tense (*Pretérito Imperfeito*)

- Regular verbs: full conjugation across persons
- Habitual past: *Eu falava, você morava...*

### ▪ Introduction to the Future Tense

- Simple future across persons:
  - *Estudarei, trabalhará, saberemos*
- Contrast with periphrastic future

### ▪ Introduction to the Subjunctive Mood

- Present subjunctive conjugation:
  - *fale, coma, abra*
- Use with different subjects: *Espero que você fale...*
